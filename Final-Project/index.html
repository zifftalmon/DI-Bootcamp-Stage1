A Timeline of Author Meir Shalev's Life:

  • 1948 - Born in Nahalal, Israel
  • 1948 to 1952 - Left with his family to Kibbutz Ginosar, and finally the family settled in Jerusalem
  • 1966 - shalev was drafted to the IDF and served in the Golani Brigade and was injured during the six-day-war (1967)
  • 1969 to 1988 - Finishes pschology school in the Hebrew University Of Jerusalem, works as a Journalist and publishes his first novel "The Blue Mountin" (in Hebrew: "Roman Rusi")
  • 1988 to present day - Shalev Continues to publish both novels and non-fiction books (Bible Now, 1985), as addition to children books (My Father Always Embarrasses Me,1988;Nehama the Louse 1990)

A quotation by Shalev concerning his writing:"The description of the bread-baking in 'Esau' and the stonemasonry in 'In his house in the desert' are based on meetings with professionals and on my continued adherence not to do what they do, but only to observe and write down lists. I never sent a dove, I did not chop a stone, I did not bake a loaf of bread and I did not buy and sell cows for meat."
